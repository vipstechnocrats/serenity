Why Selfcare?

Self-care helps reduce stress, increase resilience, and improve overall well-being.
It allows individuals to recharge, maintain balance, and prevent burnout.
Self-care practices can enhance mood and boost self-esteem,
all of which contribute to better mental health.

Meditation

Meditation can give you a sense of calm, peace and balance that can benefit your emotional well-being and your overall health. You also can use it to relax and cope with stress by focusing on something that calms you. Meditation can help you learn to stay centred and keep inner peace.

Here are some ways you can practise meditation on your own, whenever you choose:

Breathe deeply
This is good for beginners because breathing is a natural function. Focus all your attention on your breathing. Feel your breath and listen to it as you inhale and exhale through your nostrils. Breathe deeply and slowly. When your mind wanders, gently return your focus to your breathing.
Scan your body
When using this technique, focus attention on each part of your body. Become aware of how your body feels. That might be pain, tension, warmth or relaxation. Mix body scanning with breathing exercises and think about breathing heat or relaxation into and out of the parts of your body.
Walk and meditate
Meditating while walking is a good and healthy way to relax. You can use this technique anywhere you're walking, such as in a park, on a city sidewalk or at the mall.

Sleep

Sleep and health are strongly interrelated; poor sleep can increase the risk of poor health, and poor health can make it harder to sleep. Anxiety and depression often underlie sleep problems, so treating both the mental health issue and the sleep issue together is most effective. Sticking to a sleep schedule and reducing blue light exposure from devices before bedtime can help improve sleep quality. Adequate sleep helps maintain a balanced mood, reducing the risk of emotional instability, anxiety, and depression. It also supports mental clarity and decision-making, enhancing problem-solving abilities and creativity.

Exercise

Exercise is beneficial for mental health because it releases endorphins, which are natural mood lifters. It reduces stress, anxiety, and depression by promoting relaxation and improving sleep quality. Exercise also boosts self-esteem, cognitive function, and overall well-being.

Types of exercises that are particularly good for mental health include:

Aerobic exercises
Running, walking, cycling, and swimming can help improve mood and reduce anxiety.
Strength training
Lifting weights or bodyweight exercises like push-ups and squats can enhance self-esteem and reduce symptoms of depression.
Yoga and Pilates
These practices combine physical movement with mindfulness, reducing stress and promoting relaxation.
Team sports
Basketball, soccer, and other team sports provide social interaction, which can improve mood and mental health.
Mindful exercises
Tai Chi and Qigong focus on slow movements and deep breathing, helping to reduce stress and improve mental clarity.

Focus on Positivity and Stay Connected

Identify and challenge your negative and unhelpful thoughts by questioning their validity and considering alternative perspectives. Reach out to friends or family members who can provide emotional support and practical help. Talking to loved ones can offer reassurance and guidance, helping you feel less isolated and more understood. They can also provide a different viewpoint on your situation, which can be crucial in overcoming negative thinking patterns. Taking these steps can significantly improve your mental well-being and resilience.

Diet

The relationship between our diet and our mental health is complex. However, research shows a link between what we eat and how we feel. Eating well can help you feel better. You don’t have to make big changes to your diet, but see if you can try some of these tips.

Eat regularly
This can stop your blood sugar level from dropping, which can make you feel tired and bad-tempered.
Stay hydrated
Even mild dehydration can affect your mood, energy level and ability to concentrate.
Eat the right balance of fats
Your brain needs healthy fats to keep working well. They’re found in things such as olive oil, rapeseed oil, nuts, seeds, oily fish, avocados, milk and eggs. Avoid trans fats – often found in processed or packaged foods – as they can be bad for your mood and your heart health.
Include more whole grains, fruits and vegetables in your diet
They contain the vitamins and minerals your brain and body need to stay well.
Include some protein with every meal
It contains an amino acid that your brain uses to help regulate your mood.
Look after your gut health
Your gut can reflect how you’re feeling, it can speed up or slow down if you're stressed. Healthy food for your gut includes fruit, vegetables, beans and probiotics.
Be aware of how caffeine can affect your mood
It can cause sleep problems, especially if you drink it close to bedtime,and some people find it makes them irritable and anxious too. Caffeine is found in coffee, tea, cola, energy drinks and chocolate.

Practice Gratitude

Remind yourself daily of things you are grateful for, and be specific about what you appreciate. Write them down or replay them in your mind. Practicing gratitude can be a game changer, helping train your brain to notice and appreciate the little things in life. This shift in focus can transform your life experience tremendously. Regularly acknowledging the positives can increase your happiness, wellbeing, life satisfaction, and even overall health. It can also help decrease negative emotions such as anxiety, depression, and anger. By fostering a more optimistic and resilient mindset, gratitude enables you to cope better with challenges and setbacks. Consider keeping a gratitude journal where you jot down three things you're thankful for each day. This simple habit can help reinforce a positive outlook and create a sense of contentment and fulfillment. Over time, you'll likely find that this practice enhances your overall quality of life.

Set Goals And Priorities

Decide what must get done now and what can wait by prioritizing tasks based on their urgency and importance. Learn to say “no” to new tasks if you start to feel like you’re taking on too much, ensuring you don't overwhelm yourself and can maintain quality in your work. At the end of the day, take a moment to appreciate what you have accomplished, no matter how small. Recognizing your achievements can boost your morale and motivation, helping you stay focused and positive for future tasks. This practice also encourages a balanced approach to managing your responsibilities and personal well-being.
